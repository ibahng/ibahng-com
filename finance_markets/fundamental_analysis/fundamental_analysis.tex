% Defining Portfolio Manager
% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
% Portfolio managers are professionals responsible for making investment decisions on behalf of others. Active portfolio managers face a myriad of daily challenges that can be political, economic or operational in nature.

% A PM’s career:
% Finance Degree → Junior Analyst → Senior Analyst → Co-Manager → Portfolio Manager

% Investment Strategies
% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
% * Value Investing
%   * Low PE Ratio, relatively high dividend yield
%   * Prioritizes strong financial statements and lower PE relative to the market
% * Growth Investing
%   * High PE Ratio, little to no dividend yield
%   * Focuses on high earnings potential
% * GARP (Growth at a Reasonable Price)
%   * Mix of Value and Growth investing

% How a Pension Plan Invests
% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
% Large pension plans sometimes manage their own funds, but smaller plans might not have staff, and so they outsource to investment consultants who will find investment managers.
% 1. Pension plan wants to invest
% 2. Plan hires investment consultant
% 3. Consultant determines plan’s goals
% 4. Consultant finds investment managers that match plan’s goals
% 5. Consultant asks the investment managers for a Request For Proposal (RFP)
%    * RFP is a document outlining goals the consultant established w/ the pension plan in order to solicit proposals from investment managers like you
% 6. Investment managers prepare RFPs
% 7. Investment managers present RFPs to consultant
% 8. Consultant recommends the best fit investment manager to the plan
% 9. Plan invests with the investment manager

% Benchmarks
% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
% An investment manager’s job is to beat his/her benchmark which is usually an index through active management of his/her portfolio.

% Active Management vs Passive Management
% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
% Feature | Active Management | Passive Management
% ---|---|---
% Strategy | By using research-based trading and competitive info, active managers strive to beat the performance of a benchmark by generating higher returns | Passive managers seek to have an average performance by matching a given market index, rather than being it. They believe the market will outperform individual funds in the long run

% Tracking Error, VaR, and Scenarios

% Tracking Error/Volatility
% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
% This is how much a portfolio’s return deviates from the benchmark’s return.
% Factors that affect this can be anything, and what you’re trying to do here is find a common set of factors to explain the market.
% Ex. Momentum separates stocks that have outperformed and underperformed over the past year, similar to β.

% Value at Risk (VaR)
% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
% This tab quantifies the maximum loss you expect to experience with your portfolio in a worst case scenario, or “black swan” event.
% Ways to calculate VaR:
% * Monte Carlo
% * Historical
% * Parametric

% Monte Carlo
% Runs 10,000 random simulations of factor returns to determine potential loss.
% It’s very difficult to calculate this and takes a lot of computing power.

% Historical
% Uses historical factor returns to create a distribution. This method assumes that history could repeat itself. But as we all know, that doesn’t always happen. Past performance is not indicative of future returns.

% Parametric
% Assumes a normal distribution of returns. However actual portfolio returns don’t tend to fall in a neat, normal distribution. This method often underestimates how much you could lose.

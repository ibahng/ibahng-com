== FACTORS OF PRODUCTION

Economic System: Mechanism for deciding what to make, how to make it, and who gets it

Market Economy: Individuals make decisions and keep what they produce (protected private property rights), prices set by supply and demand (CAPITALISM)
Grows the economy through an invisible hand
Problems: Inequality, nonoptimal business practices

Command Economy: Government makes decisions and sets goals, no private property rights (COMMUNISM?)
Problems: Lack of incentive for investing, improving quality, and innovating

== OPPORTUNITY COST

Law of Increasing Opportunity Cost
  - As more and more of a good is produced, the opportunity cost of producing the good increases
  - Reason: The resources that are best suited for producign a good are used first, and once they are used, producing more requires resources that are less suited for producing it, so more resources are required

Cost-Benefit Analysis
  Individuals and businesses make the best decisions by maximizing difference between total benefits and total costs

  Costs should include both explicit and implicit (opportunity) costs

  Example Problem: Adam decides to start a business. He spends \$200,000 in expenses to start the business, and earns \$255,000 in revenue during the first year. If he didn't start the business, he could’ve instead worked as an accountant earning a salary of \$50,000 and placed the \$200,000 he spent in a savings account with an annual interest rate of 3%.

  What are the explicit and implicit costs of running the business for a year?

  Explicit costs: 200,000 spent to start business

  Implicit Costs
  - Forgone Salary: 50,000
  - Forgone Interest: 0.03 x 200,000 = 6,000
  - Total Implicit Costs: 56,000

  Accounting vs Economic Profit
  - Accounting profit = revenue - explicit costs
  - Economic profit = revenue - explicit and implicit costs

== SUPPLY AND SUPPLY DETERMINANTS

Elasticity
- Measures how responsive something is to a change in something else
- Price Elasticity of Demand: Measures how much quantity demanded changes in response to changes in price

\[
E_D = \left| \frac{\%\Delta Q_{\text{demanded}}}{\%\Delta P} \right| = \left| \frac{\frac{Q_{\text{new}} - Q_{\text{old}}}{Q_{\text{old}}}}{\frac{P_{\text{new}} - P_{\text{old}}}{P_{\text{old}}}} \right|
\]

Determinants of Price Elasticity of Demand
- Substitutes
    More substitutes available = more elastic
    more similar substitutes = more elastic
- Want vs Need
    need = more elastic
- Timeframe
    short term = less elastic
- share of income
    lower share of income = less elastic

Describing Elasticity
- a horizontal demand curve is perfectly elastic, meaning that price increases will lead to zero demand and price decreases will lead to infinite demand
- an elastic coefficient between 1 and infinity is elastic
- "" at 1 is unit elastic
- "" between 0 and 1 is inelastic
- "" at 0 is perfectly inelastic (vertical demand curve)

Total revenue test
- if revenue decreases as price and quantity increases, elastic
- if revenue doesn't change as price and quantity change, unit elastic
- if revenue increases as price and quantity increases, inelastic

the total revenue test also implies that a standard downward sloping demand curve is elastic towards the top left, unit elastic in the middle, and inelastic on the bottom right

Price Elasticity of Supply: Measures how much quantity supplied changes in response to changes in price 
\[
E_S = \frac{\%\Delta Q_{\text{supplied}}}{\%\Delta P} = \frac{\frac{Q_{\text{new}} - Q_{\text{old}}}{Q_{\text{old}}}}{\frac{P_{\text{new}} - P_{\text{old}}}{P_{\text{old}}}}
\]

Determinants of Price Elasticity of Supply
- Timeframe
    short term = less elastic

Other Elasticities

- Income Elasticity of Demand: measures the elasticity of a good based on the income level of consumers
    if positive, good is normal
    if negative, good is inferior

- Cross Elasticity of Demand: measures the elasticity of a good compared to levels of a substitute of complementary product
    if positive, x and y are substitutes
    if negative, x and y are complementary
